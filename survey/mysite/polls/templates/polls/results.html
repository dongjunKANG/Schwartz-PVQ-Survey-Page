Detailed Value Description


Universalism
Values understanding, appreciation, tolerance, and protection for the welfare of all people and for nature

  • Key words : Broadminded, Equality, Harmony, Wisdom, Moral, Peace, Compassion
  • Yes-ex : Sustainability is important for the welfare of everyone in the world.
  • No-ex : As a teacher, I give my favorite students special treatment and better grades.


    • Benevolence
    Values preserving and enhancing the welfare of those with whom one is in frequent personal contact (the ‘in-group’)

  • Key words : Helpful, Friendship, Forgiving, Kindness, Charitable, Concerning
  • Yes-ex : I often check in with friends/family to make sure they are in good health.
  • No-ex : Sometimes I take my brother’s lunch on purpose so he has nothing to eat.


    • Conformity
    Values restraint of actions, inclinations, and impulses likely to upset or harm others and violate social expectations or norms

  • Key words : Obedient, Respectful, Norms, Principle, Compliant, Discipline
  • Yes-ex : I never cheat on homework because I’m afraid to get caught and punished.
  • No-ex : I snuck out after my mom told me I was grounded.


    • Tradition
    Values respect, commitment, and acceptance of the customs and ideas that one’s culture or religion provides

  • Key words : Humble, Devout, Religion, Responsibility, Classic
  • Yes-ex : When I visit my family, I always pay my respects to my ancestors.
  • No-ex : I no longer follow the religion I was raised with so I don’t go to church.


    • Security
    Values safety, harmony, and stability of society, of relationships, and of self

  • Key words : Healthy, Family, Safety, Belonging, Welfare, Job, Safekeeping, Support
  • Yes-ex : I support the new laws because they protect my family.
  • No-ex : I support the new laws that give the government permission to track you.


    • Power
    Values social status and prestige, control or dominance over people and resources

  • Key words : Wealth, Authority, Recognition, Leadership, Influence, Investment
  • Yes-ex : I want to run for office because I have new potential changes.
  • No-ex : I don’t want to be famous because I don’t like people watching me.


    • Achievement
    Values personal success through demonstrating competence according to social standards

  • Key words : Influential, Successful, Ambitious, Talented, Intelligent, Challenge, Performance
  • Yes-ex : I worked everyday to complete my goal of buying a car.
  • No-ex : I expect to earn something even if I did not work for it.


    • Hedonism
    Values pleasure or sensuous gratification for oneself

  • Key words : Enjoy, Happiness, Amusement, Satisfaction, Relax, Exercise
  • Yes-ex : I’d rather do something that I enjoy, than do homework.
  • No-ex : I want to go out with my friends, but I have to clean my car.


    • Stimulation
    Values excitement, novelty, and challenge in life

  • Key words : Daring, Variation, Courage, Adventure, Intense, Possibilities, Nervous
  • Yes-ex : We should try skydiving!
  • No-ex : Deepsea diving sounds scary, maybe we should stay in instead.


    • Self-direction
    Values independent thought and action–choosing, creating, exploring

  • Key words : Curious, Independent, Self-reliance, Discover, Identity
  • Yes-ex : Even though none of my friends want to go, I’m going to France.
  • No-ex : I can’t imagine leaving my hometown because all my siblings are staying.
